🎲 Probability Basics

What is Probability?

Probability is a number that tells you how likely something is to happen. It's always between 0 (impossible) and 1 (certain).

Think of it like a weather forecast: "There's a 70% chance of rain." That's probability — the chance is 0.70 (or 70 out of 100).

The Probability Formula
P(event) = Favorable outcomes ÷ Total possible outcomes
How many ways can it happen? ÷ How many total things could happen?

Complement: The Opposite Event

The complement of an event is "everything ELSE" — the probability that the event does NOT happen.

Complement Rule
P(NOT A) = 1 − P(A)
If P(rain) = 0.3, then P(no rain) = 1 − 0.3 = 0.7
P(rolling a 6)
1/6

5/6
🎯 Quick Trick: If a problem asks "what's the probability something does NOT happen," just calculate the probability it DOES happen and subtract from 1!

Independent vs Dependent Events

Independent 🎲🎲
One event does NOT affect the other.
Rolling a die twice — the first roll doesn't change the second.
Flipping a coin after rolling a die.
Two separate coin flips.
Dependent 🎒→📘
One event DOES affect the other.
Drawing cards WITHOUT replacing — fewer cards remain!
Picking marbles from a bag without putting them back.
Eating a candy from a box then picking another.
📐 Probability Rules

AND Rule (Multiplication) — Both Events Happen

What's the probability of BOTH events happening? Multiply their probabilities!

AND Rule (Independent Events)
P(A AND B) = P(A) × P(B)
Example: Coin AND Die
What's the probability of flipping Heads AND rolling a 6?
P(Heads) = 1/2
P(6) = 1/6
P(Heads AND 6) = 1/2 × 1/6 = 1/12 ≈ 0.083 (8.3%)
# Probability of heads AND rolling 6 p_heads = 1/2 p_six = 1/6 p_both = p_heads * p_six print(f"P(Heads AND 6) = {p_heads} × {p_six} = {p_both:.4f}") print(f"That's about {p_both*100:.1f}% chance")
P(Heads AND 6) = 0.5 × 0.16667 = 0.0833 That's about 8.3% chance

OR Rule (Addition) — Either Event Happens

What's the probability of AT LEAST ONE event happening? Add their probabilities (but don't double-count!).

OR Rule
P(A OR B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A AND B)
Subtract the overlap so you don't count it twice!
Mutually Exclusive (no overlap)
Rolling a 2 OR a 5?
Can't be BOTH at once!
P(2 or 5) = 1/6 + 1/6 = 2/6 = 1/3
With Overlap
Drawing a King OR a Heart?
King of Hearts is BOTH!
P = 4/52 + 13/52 − 1/52 = 16/52

🔗 Conditional Probability

What is Conditional Probability?

Conditional probability is the chance of something happening GIVEN that something else has already happened. The key word is "given".

Think of it like this: "What's the probability of getting an A on the test, given that you studied?" That's different from the overall probability of getting an A!

Conditional Probability Formula
P(A | B) = P(A and B) ÷ P(B)
Read as: "Probability of A given B"
The | symbol means "given that"

Simple Example: Colored Marbles

A bag has 10 marbles: 5 red (3 big, 2 small) and 5 blue (1 big, 4 small).

Question: You pick a marble and it's RED. What's the probability it's also BIG?
In math: P(Big | Red) = ?
Step 1: Since we KNOW it's red, we only look at the 5 red marbles.
Step 2: Of the 5 red marbles, 3 are big.
Answer: P(Big | Red) = 3/5 = 0.6 = 60%
🔑 Key Insight: The "given" information shrinks your world. Instead of looking at ALL 10 marbles, you only look at the 5 red ones. The "given" becomes your new total!

Real-World Example: Student Grades

A class has 30 students. Here's the data:

Studied ✅ Didn't Study ❌ Total
Passed ✅ 18 2 20
Failed ❌ 2 8 10
Total 20 10 30
P(Passed | Studied)
Of the 20 who studied, 18 passed → 18/20 = 90%!
P(Passed | Didn't Study)
Of the 10 who didn't study, only 2 passed → 2/10 = 20%
P(Passed) overall
Out of all 30 students, 20 passed → 20/30 = 66.7%
# Conditional probability in Python studied_and_passed = 18 total_studied = 20 p_pass_given_study = studied_and_passed / total_studied print(f"P(Pass | Studied) = {studied_and_passed}/{total_studied} = {p_pass_given_study:.0%}") not_studied_and_passed = 2 total_not_studied = 10 p_pass_given_no_study = not_studied_and_passed / total_not_studied print(f"P(Pass | Didn't Study) = {not_studied_and_passed}/{total_not_studied} = {p_pass_given_no_study:.0%}")
P(Pass | Studied) = 18/20 = 90% P(Pass | Didn't Study) = 2/10 = 20%

Machine Learning is built on conditional probability! Every prediction an AI makes is essentially asking: "Given what I see, what's the probability of each outcome?"
